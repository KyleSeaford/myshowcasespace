# Three Portfolio Homepage Mistakes We See Repeatedly

A homepage does not need to say everything. It needs to make the next click feel easy.

## 1. Leading with clutter

When a page opens with too many competing blocks, visitors stop scanning and start skipping.

The first screen should make it obvious who the creator is, what kind of work they do, and where to go next.

## 2. Writing vague intros

A headline like "Welcome to my creative world" does not tell a client, curator, or collaborator much.

Specific beats generic almost every time.

Try a clearer angle:

- selected editorial photography
- identity systems for modern brands
- mixed-media work focused on memory and place

## 3. Hiding contact intent

If someone likes the work, the next question is usually practical: can I reach this person?

That means contact details and collaboration context should be visible and easy to trust.

Good portfolio pages do not oversell. They reduce friction.
